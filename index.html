June 14, 2026 · Micah 6:8

Daily Christ-orientation check

“He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the Lord require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God.” — Micah 6:8, NIV

Micah speaks into Israel’s covenant life, where outward religion could not replace justice, mercy, and humble faithfulness. In Jesus, we see this fully embodied: truth without cruelty, mercy without compromise, and humble obedience that serves rather than performs.

  1. Where today do I need to choose justice in a concrete way—not just good intentions, but an action that protects, repairs, or tells the truth?
  2. Is my walk with God marked by humble dependence on Jesus, or am I trying to look faithful while staying in control?

Lord Jesus, form in me a life that is truthful, merciful, and humbly surrendered to You.

June 14, 2026 · AI and Cybersecurity

AI Scams Are a Trust Problem Before They Are a Tech Problem

Source: Google Safety & Security on combating AI scams

Google published an update on its efforts to fight AI-powered scams, including a lawsuit aimed at a cybercrime network it calls the “Outsider Enterprise.” According to Google, the group uses phishing kits, fake websites, brand impersonation, and mass text-message campaigns to steal passwords, payment information, and money.

The numbers are sobering. Google says the operation is connected to 9,000 fake websites, more than 1 million fraudulent URLs, and millions of scam messages. In a two-week period in May 2026, Android users flagged 55,000 spam texts, while Google says 2.5 million related messages were sent to Android users.

This is where the AI conversation has to grow up. AI is not only a productivity tool or a shiny assistant. In the hands of criminals, it can make deception cheaper, faster, and more convincing. Fake package alerts, urgent bank warnings, and “your account is compromised” messages are not just annoying. They are attacks on trust.

Scams work by rushing people. They create fear, pressure, and confusion so someone acts before they have time to think. That matters because the people most at risk are often the tired, distracted, elderly, overwhelmed, or trusting — the very people security should be designed to protect.

Google’s response combines legal action, FBI coordination, telecom partnerships, product-level protections, and legislative advocacy. That layered approach matters because no single person can solve this alone. Users need better tools. Companies need accountability. Law enforcement needs coordination. Families, churches, schools, and communities need plain-language education.

A few practical reminders still matter: do not click urgent links from unexpected texts, do not enter passwords through message links, verify directly through the official app or website, and slow down when a message tries to create panic. Urgency is often the attacker’s favorite weapon.

AI may change the scale of scams, but the heart of the issue is old: deception for gain. The response should be truthful, protective, patient, and wise.
