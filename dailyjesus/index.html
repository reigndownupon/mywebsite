June 19, 2026 · 2 Corinthians 5:17

New Creation in Christ

“Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!” 2 Corinthians 5:17, NIV

Paul announces that union with Christ is not a small improvement project, but the arrival of new creation life. Because Jesus died and rose, today can be lived from His renewing mercy rather than from the old patterns that once named us.

  1. What old label, habit, or fear am I still letting define me more than my life in Christ?
  2. Where can I practice one visible sign of new creation today, such as reconciliation, honesty, generosity, or patient love?

Lord Jesus, help me live today as someone made new in You.

June 18, 2026 · Hebrews 12:1–2

Eyes Fixed on Jesus

“And let us run with perseverance the race marked out for us, fixing our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and perfecter of faith.” Hebrews 12:1–2, NIV

Hebrews calls weary believers to endurance by looking at Jesus, who opened and completed the way of faith through costly love. The image is not a solo sprint powered by self-improvement, but a persevering race surrounded by witnesses and freed from the sin and weight that slow us down.

  1. What weight am I carrying today that keeps pulling my eyes away from Jesus?
  2. What would it look like to take one faithful next step with endurance rather than hurry, despair, or distraction?

Lord Jesus, steady my attention on You and teach me to run with faithful endurance.

June 17, 2026 · Galatians 5:22–23

Fruit the Spirit Grows

“But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control.” Galatians 5:22–23, NIV

Paul names the Spirit’s fruit in contrast to desires that fracture people and communities. This fruit is not a personality performance or a religious mask. It is the visible character of life surrendered to Christ, growing through the Spirit in ordinary choices, conversations, frustrations, and acts of love.

  1. Which part of the Spirit’s fruit do I most need to welcome today in my words, reactions, or relationships?
  2. Where can I cooperate with the Spirit by choosing patient love instead of the old patterns that divide?

Holy Spirit, grow the character of Jesus in me today, especially where my own strength runs thin.
